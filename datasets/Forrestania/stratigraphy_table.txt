  - NO density, magnetic susceptibility, or resistivity values are reported in the paper. Proxy minerals are noted instead.
  - "Mag" = magnetic mineral present; "Cond" = conductive mineral present; "—" = no data / not applicable.

============================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================
METAMORPHIC & STRUCTURAL SUMMARY
============================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================
Peak metamorphism:      655 ± 30°C, 4.0 ± 1.0 kbar (upper amphibolite facies)
S1a regional foliation: Dip ~62° → 090° (N-striking, layer-parallel)
S1b (flattened zones):  Dip ~72–79° → 092° (at FW sediment/ore/komatiite and basalt/HW sediment contacts)
F2 shear zones:         Dip ~70–75° → 092°; normal dip-slip; L1 mineral lineation plunges 62–79° → east
F3 thrusts:             Dip ≤10° → SE (up to 30° at depth); reverse; segment orebody into 11 shoots; ≤300 m offset
F4 faults:              Dip ~60° → east; brittle-ductile normal; granite contact; hosts T0/T1 upper + T4 upper ore
D5 brittle faults:      Dip ~82° → 342°; strike-slip + minor normal; ≤5 m offset
Granite emplacement:    D4, post-peak-metamorphism, exploited F2/F3; pooled at "triple points"; back-rotated stratigraphy
Dolerite dike:          D5, ca. 2.4 Ga Widgiemooltha swarm, E-W trend; locally intrudes along F2

============================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================
PRACTICAL NOTES FOR GEOPHYSICAL INVERSION / FORWARD MODELLING
============================================================================================================================================================================================================================================================
1. SURFACE COVER: Granite from 0 to ~120 m (and deeper along faults). Deep weathering throughout. Any surface geophysics
   must account for a thick resistive granite cap and weathered overburden.

2. CONDUCTIVITY TARGETS (strongest → weakest):
   - Massive/breccia/stringer Ni sulfides (pyrrhotite-dominant, very conductive, but thin: 2–20 m)
   - Hanging-wall sediments (graphite-bearing — potentially a significant stratiform conductor)
   - Interflow & FW sediments with pyrite (weakly conductive, thin <5 m)

3. MAGNETIC TARGETS:
   - Serpentinized ultramafics (serpentine + magnetite after olivine) — komatiites and komatiitic basalts
   - Massive sulfides (pyrrhotite + magnetite)
   - Granite (variable magnetite content)
   - Tholeiitic basalts (magnetite-bearing)
   - Interflow sediments (magnetite-bearing)

4. DENSITY CONTRASTS:
   - Highest: massive sulfides (>>4.0 g/cm³) and disseminated sulfides (≥3.5)
   - High: komatiites, pyroxenite, basalts (2.80–3.10+)
   - Moderate: sedimentary rocks (2.65–2.80), granite (2.60–2.75)
   - Lowest: overburden/regolith

5. KEY CAVEATS:
   - The stratigraphy is NOT a simple layer cake. F3 thrusts duplicate/omit sections, and granite intrudes along
     fault planes deep into the sequence.
   - Ore shoots are thin (2–5 m typically), steeply dipping, and plunging shallowly SE. They will NOT appear as
     thick conductive bodies in EM/MT — likely thin conductor plates.
   - The granite-FW sediment contact (F4 fault) hosts some of the largest ore shoots (T0/T1 upper, T4 upper).
   - HW sediments with graphite are a potential false-positive EM target (conductive but barren).
   - The dolerite dike crosscuts everything — a late, magnetically distinct linear feature.
