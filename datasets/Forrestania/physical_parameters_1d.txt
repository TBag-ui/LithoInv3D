==============================================================================================================
STRUCTURALLY EMPLACED REPEAT UNITS (not in stratigraphic order — granite-hosted ore along faults)
==============================================================================================================

Granite-hosted Massive Sulfides (T0/T1 upper, T4 upper):
  - At granite / FW-sedimentary rock contact (F4 fault, dip ~60°E)
  - Depth variable: ~500–1000 m from surface (~800–400 mRL)
  - Same physical parameters as massive sulfides above (ρ 3.80–4.50, κ 50–250+, Q 2–15)
  - Breccia texture: granite clasts in sulfide matrix or vice versa
  - Distinctive: ore ENTIRELY SEPARATED from ultramafic host

T5 Pipe (granite-encapsulated ore):
  - Massive sulfides almost entirely enveloped by granite + pegmatite
  - Offset ~20m east of main T5 shoot along F3 shear
  - ≤20m wide, ~230m strike extent
  - Same physical parameters as massive sulfides

Stringer & Vein Sulfides (in FW sediments):
  - ≤3m from komatiite/FW-sediment contact; mm to cm scale
  - Density only slightly elevated from host FW sediments
  - Likely sub-resolution for surface geophysics unless dense vein network

==============================================================================================================
ESTIMATION BASIS
==============================================================================================================
Density:         Mineral assemblage → typical rock densities from literature (e.g., pyrrhotite 4.6, pentlandite 5.0,
                 pyrite 5.0, olivine 3.2-3.4, tremolite 2.9-3.2, plagioclase 2.6-2.8, quartz 2.65, biotite 2.8-3.1)

Mag Suscept:     Mineral assemblage → typical SI ranges (e.g., magnetite 1000-5000, monoclinic pyrrhotite 1-10,
                 hexagonal pyrrhotite ~0.1, pyrite 0.1, silicates ~0). Komatiite values elevated due to
                 serpentinization (olivine → serpentine + magnetite). Granite variable (± magnetite).

Remanence (Q):   Koenigsberger ratio (M_rem / M_ind). Pyrrhotite-rich massive sulfides can have Q >> 1.
                 Serpentinized ultramafics can carry moderate remanence (magnetite after olivine).
                 Proterozoic dolerite dike may carry strong thermal remanence (not separately listed —
                 crosscuts everything, E-W trend, similar parameters to basalt with higher remanence).

==============================================================================================================
CAVEATS FOR GEOPHYSICAL MODELLING
==============================================================================================================
1.  This 1D column is a SIMPLIFICATION. The real geometry is complex: F3 thrusts duplicate/omit sections,
    granite intrudes along faults to variable depths, and ore shoots are offset up to 300m.

2.  The ore shoots are thin (2–20m) and steeply dipping. Surface geophysics will see them as thin,
    near-vertical conductive/magnetic plates, not thick horizontal layers.

3.  Graphite in HW sediments is a potential false-positive conductor — it may look like ore in EM/MT data.

4.  Remanence direction in pyrrhotite ore may differ significantly from the present-day field,
    especially given the complex deformation and thermal history.

5.  ALL numbers are estimates. They should be treated as priors for inversion, not hard constraints.
    Seek petrophysical measurements from drill core before committing to a final model.
